User Persona

  • Name: Barbara Adams
  • Age: 63
  • Occupation: Retired English Teacher
  • Hobbies: Gardening, spending time with grandchildren, crafting greeting cards for local artisan craft fairs, going out outdoors with her two black labs Rico and Lady (dogs).
  • Technology: 2015 Samsung Galaxy j7 , 2017 Dell E6430 Laptop
  • Frequency of Use Not familiar or frequent user of technology. Generally uses the internet for banking or quick searches. She owns a smart phone but only because it was recommended by her daughter
  • Attitudes and behaviors: Barbara is a huge fan of hiking and being outdoors, but is not adept at using technology. She will not use a website that seems complicated or hard to use. She wants to quickly be able to view sites and hopes to find one that she can save hikes to do or hikes she has already done.
  • Frustrations and needs: Barbara doesn't have a way to quickly find new hiking and walking trails in the area. She doesn't want to use a conventional map but also isn't a big user or fan of complicated technology. She wants to be able to search and potentially save hiking trails digitally. She would also like to be able to tell if the trail is dog friendly.
  • Goals:Barbara's main concern is finding a way to digitally view and save hiking trails in her area that are dog friendly. She wants to quickly find a new trail to hike without having to use a conventional map.

User Story

Barbara searches for dog-friendly hiking trail in Albuquerque

Use Case

  • Title: Barbara uses web application to find hiking trail
  • Description: Barbara uses her smart phone to look for hiking trails in Albuquerque.
  • Usage Pre-conditions: Must have mobile phone
  • Usage Post-conditions: The application shows Barbara hiking trails in the area and directions to the trail.

Interaction Flow

  • Barbara opens up Google Chrome on her smart phone and types ABQ Trails into her navigation bar
  • The browser returns the homepage of the website, which immediately opens pop-up for an optional sign-in/sign-up
  • Barbara does not wish to create an account and doesn't currently have one so she closes the pop up
  • ABQ Trails closes the window and displays the homepage which includes a list with photos of top trails and
